For example, \(q_1(x,y)=4 x^2-4xy+4y^2\) and \(q_2(x,y,z)=9x^2-4 y^2-4xy-2xz+z^2\) are quadratic forms. Note that each term in a quadratic form is of degree two. We omit linear terms, since these can be absorbed by completing the square. The important observation is that every quadratic form can be associated to a symmetric matrix.
