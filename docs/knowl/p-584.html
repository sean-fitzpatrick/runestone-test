The overall structure therefore looks like \((A\Rightarrow B)\Rightarrow (C\Rightarrow D)\text{.}\) A direct proof should be structured as follows:

  1. Assume the main hypothesis: \(A\Rightarrow B\text{.}\)

  2. Assume the “sub”-hypothesis \(C\text{.}\)

  3. Figure out how to show that \(C\Rightarrow A\text{.}\) (This is the “apply \(T\) to both sides” step.)

  4. If we know \(A\text{,}\) and we've assumed \(A\Rightarrow B\text{,}\) we know \(B\text{.}\)

  5. Realize that \(B\Rightarrow D\text{.}\)
