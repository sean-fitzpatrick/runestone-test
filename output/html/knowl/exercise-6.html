Exercise 1.1.10.

By rearranging the blocks below, create a proof of the statement “Let \(V\) be a vector space. For any \(\vv\in V\text{,}\) \((-1)\vv=-\vv\text{.}\)” Note that it's possible that not all blocks will be needed.

Let \(\vv\) be any vector in \(V\text{.}\)

---

Since \(-1+1=0\text{,}\) \((-1+1)\vv=0\vv\text{.}\)

---

From Exercise 1.1.9 we know \(0\vv=\zer\text{,}\) and by axiom S3, \((-1+1)\vv=-1\vv+1\vv\text{.}\)

---

By axiom S5, \(1\vv=\vv\text{.}\)

---

Therefore, we have \(-1\vv+\vv=\zer\text{.}\)

---

Adding \(-\vv\) to both sides, we get \((-1\vv+\vv)+(-\vv)=\zer+(-\vv)\text{.}\)

---

Using A3 and A4, this becomes \(-1\vv+(\vv+(-\vv))=-\vv\text{.}\)

---

By A5, we then have \(-1\vv+\zer=-\vv\text{.}\)

---

Finally, by A4, we get \(-1\vv=-\vv\text{.}\)
