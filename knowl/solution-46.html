Solution 3.1.6.1.

If \(\xx=\zer\text{,}\) then the result follows immediately from the dot product formula in DefinitionÂ 3.1.1. Conversely, suppose \(\xx\dotp \vv_i=0\) for each \(i\text{.}\) Since the \(\vv_i\) span \(\R^n\text{,}\) there must exist scalars \(c_1,c_2,\ldots, c_k\) such that \(\xx=c_1\vv_1+c_2\vv_2+\cdots+c_k\vv_k\text{.}\) But then

\begin{align*} \xx\dotp\xx \amp = \xx\dotp (c_1\vv_1+c_2\vv_2+\cdots+c_k\vv_k) \\ \amp = c_1(\xx\dotp \vv_1)+ c_2(\xx\dotp \vv_2)+\cdots +c_k(\xx\dotp \vv_k)\\ \amp = c_1(0)+c_2(0)+\cdots + c_k(0)=0\text{.} \end{align*}
