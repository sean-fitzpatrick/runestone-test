Theorem 5.5.3.

Let \(V\) be a complex vector space, and let \(T:V\to V\) be a linear operator. (We can take \(V\) to be real if we assume that \(T\) has all real eigenvalues.) Let \(\lambda_1,\ldots, \lambda_k\) be the distinct eigenvalues of \(T\text{.}\) Then each generalized eigenspace \(G_{\lambda_j}(T)\) is \(T\)-invariant, and we have the direct sum decomposition

\begin{equation*} V = G_{\lambda_1}(T)\oplus G_{\lambda_2}(T)\oplus \cdots \oplus G_{\lambda_k}(T)\text{.} \end{equation*}
