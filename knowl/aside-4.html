A note on notation. Given linear maps \(U\xrightarrow{T} V\xrightarrow{S} W\text{,}\) we typically write the composition \(S\circ T:U\to W\) as a “product” \(ST\text{.}\) The reason for this is again to mimic the case of matrices: as seen in Remark 2.3.10, \(T_A\circ T_B = T_{AB}\) for matrix transformations.
